Smart Crack Detection & Water Ingress Monitoring

Integrated Health Monitoring for RCC and SFRC Specimens

📌 Overview

Research conducted at GEC Thrissur on Structural Health Monitoring (SHM). The project focuses on early-stage damage detection in concrete using advanced sensing to prevent structural failure and deterioration.

⚙️ Objective

  • Monitor crack initiation using Fiber Optic Sensors (DFOS)
  • Detect water ingress via capacitance-based sensing
  • Compare RCC and Steel Fiber Reinforced Concrete (SFRC)

🧠 Methodology

Specimens underwent three-point bending tests to analyze "fiber-bridging" mechanisms. Integrated capacitance sensors were embedded to establish relationships between permeability and moisture levels.

🛠️ Technologies Used

  • Distributed Fiber Optic Sensors (DFOS)
  • Capacitance-Based Moisture Sensors
  • Experimental UTM Testing
  • Online Monitoring Systems

🚀 Key Findings

  • Steel fibers significantly enhance crack resistance
  • DFOS allows real-time crack propagation tracking
  • Improved distribution of cracks in SFRC specimens
  • Effective correlation of moisture to sensor data

📊 Applications

  • Civil Infrastructure Monitoring
  • Bridges and High-Rise Durability
  • Predictive Maintenance for Concrete
  • Smart City Infrastructure
